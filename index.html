J. Luna Zagorac

/jɒvənə ˈluːnə zɑgorəts/ | يوفانا لونا زاغوراتس | Јована Луна Загорац

I am a fifth year Ph.D. Candidate in Physics at Yale University primarily working on theoretical particle cosmology and HPC simulations of dark matter. In particular, my graduate thesis is focused on HPC simulations and observational signatures of ultralight (or fuzzy) dark matter. I am co-advised by Nikhil Padmanabhan at Yale and Richard Easther at the University of Auckland in New Zealand. I am also a Future Investigator in NASA Earth and Space Science and Technology (FINESST) and a Fellow of the Franke Program in Science and the Humanities at Yale, which supports my work on mapping Ancient Egyptian diagonal star tables to observational astronomical data.

Apart from research I enjoy science communications, particularly through writing for Astrobites and outreach. I also love teaching, and have dabbled in curriculum development for the Intro Physics Bootcamp for incoming graduate students at Yale. I thrive at the intersection of the sciences and the humanities, and like learning languages: ancient, modern, and computer. When not doing any of the above, I buy too many books (and even read some of them!), drink dangerous amount of caffeine, play video games that make me nostalgic for the mid-00's, and roller skate.
